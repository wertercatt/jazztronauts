Jazztronauts

Probably the last gmod 1 gamemode we will make

Summary

Jazztronauts is a celebration of 15+ years of user made content in the source community. The goal is to explore and find items in randomly selected user levels, using a suite of custom tools and mechanics to enable the player a greater variety of options to traverse any given level. Upon completion of all the goals in a given level, the players will return to a hub world called The Bar where they can level up their tools and acquire new assets to further help explore and create fun scenarios to just generally have a good time looking at weird old bsp files that may be otherwise never played seriously again. Jazztronauts has a simple story and cast of new characters to help players get into the mindset of exploring these levels for a purpose, serving to eventually get far enough to see An Ending.

Design Tenets

Reward the player for being clever and using mechanics in an unexpected way

Embrace how janky the engine is, and allow it to break down.

The treadmill is there to give the illusion of progression, NOT to limit the player

Make moving around in a space fun, don’t make it a shortcut.

Major states of Jazztronauts:

Opening

The game opens with a fake garry’s mod horror map. It is deceptively standard fare for gmod, and the scares should be very ineffective if present at all. The entire time if the player looks and observes the environment carefully there will, at points, be early hints of what’s going on in the form of silhouettes running across catwalks or other very subtle visual cues.

At the halfway point the players will witness a new toolgun of some sort fall into the room they are standing in, landing in a clearly visible and telegraphed area inviting them to pick it up. At this point they have the kidnapping tool and we let them use it without explaining anything for a few moments so they can puzzle out how it works, but not WHAT it does. This stage is important because this is the only chance we will get to give them a controlled scenario to experiment with the gun, and as long as they get some sort of feel for it then we’ve won.

So now they have this new gun, further down the road they come to a long hallway with a cargobay visible behind an open shutter at the end of the hall. As they approach, a strange bus crashes through the wall out of view and comes skidding to a halt, framed by the shutter at the end of the hall. A few moments later, a cat person jumps into view and is seen searching the room for something. As she scans the room, she locks eyes with the player and shouts to unseen companions, followed by 3 more cats appearing into view. They run towards the player and via a Trigger volume we capture them and force them into a cutscene.

The cutscene goes from a hl1 callback where gordon gets kidnapped by hgrunts, to a scene of them talking next to the bus, to the player on the bus with the cats happily chatting about something. At this 3rd vignette the player is no longer groggy and gets to witness the bus driving into a portal where the wall is broken in first person. Fade to white, title card. Smash cut to scenes of them driving through a jazz void while Que Chevera plays, use this opportunity to sell the mood and give in-universe title cards.

Main Game Loop

The player wakes up in a stage area connected to the Bar and are forced to watch the cats explain what they are doing there, perhaps again through a forced cutscene via hammer, or the Jazztronauts dialogue system (see mechanics scope). After they leave the theater and enter the bar proper we need to make sure they at least interact with the shop systems at least once and understand where everything is, and give them some free money to get started. Check What’s In The Hub? For context of what rooms exist.

Now that introductions are out of the way it's just normal Jazztronauts loop until the end of game. The loop consists of returning home, going to level up their tools at the shop, restocking exploration assets, and checking to see if they’ve unlocked any conversation with one of the 4 jazz cats. We’ll call this the buy phase from now on. At any point during the buy phase a player may go out to the bus station and reroll the next map that the group will go to, once they find a promising one everyone may go about their business until they are ready to explore, in which case they all physically get on the bus and once we see every player there the bus drives off and we have a vignette of the bus on a highway in the jazz dimension, and we give them as much info about the map as possible. This extra stage exists to give hype them up and give them one last chance to cancel and get anything they forgot in the buy phase. After an amount of time the bus drives off into the distance and we load the next map.

Next comes the Exploration Phase, upon loading I suggest we do a mario sunshine style camera panning over the map so they can get a really fast idea of what the layout looks like, and any landmarks they can spot (see polish scope). Upon loading we programmatically scatter 5 Orb Shards, and the immediate goal is to collect all 5 shards. Upon collection of them, The Main Orb spawns in a random spot that we telegraph with a smash cut that all players are forced to view. This hurdle exists as a test to see how well they understand the levels layout, and if they can reach it then they’ve won the map and the bus arrives to drive them back home.

Aside from collecting orbs, during the exploration phase the players may steal any arbitrary props, npcs, and other entities from the map using a toolgun. The stolen assets are either broken down into money, or added to a collection room of some sort (see mechanics scope/polish scope). Players should be incentivized to steal assets from a level out of entertainment value, and to allow them to buy more dumb garbage to use in maps and have fun with, while allowing them a greater degree of agency over their own movement. Aside from blanket stealing props, the players have a checklist of things they need to get to see a given Jazz Cat Dialogue Event (see sunabouzu’s shameful dating sim corner). Any item stolen will first go towards furthering jazz cat events, and if the thing they stole isn't needed for any event, then it goes towards normal asset rules. A preliminary list of exploration tools and assets can be found at Mechanics Scope. 

At the end of the exploration phase we have another bus vignette just like the one they used to confirm the map for the last time, a bus in a jazz dimension. Here though we show how much money everyone got, if the mission was successful, and any other humorous metrics we can track (like most kleiners stolen or something). After an amount of time, the bus drives off into the distance and we go back to the start of the loop and it happens all over again.

Finale

After a specific amount of Orbs are collected, the number which will be determined based on playtesting and developer opinion, the finale will be triggered and the next map will be force-set to the finale bsp. The finale is one cutscene, where the bus falls off the jazz highway and all the players are free floating down into darkness. After a worrying amount of time, we see an infinity cube framed behind a grand display of light and power. There, is a prog band playing the Eternal Set Of Existence, which is the longest song in the known universe and also caused the big bang. The wizened band members have a dialogue, and eventually they give the players some shitty non-advice. Smash cut to the players floating unimpressed in the void, bathed in light, as the jazz cats float around mewling and enjoying the festival. The camera cuts back to the band, as they sigh and agree to tell them The Secret. Music swells, and the camera points up to a single image, framed in holy light: “Don’t be an asshole”. A simple, pretty true message, and the camera fades to black. See Polish Scope  for ideas on what to do after the main game loop is finished.

Whats in the hub?

The Bar has only a few rooms, to keep asset production smaller and to keep the buy phase from taking too long. A list of all rooms confirmed for production are:

-The Bus Stop: The bus that delivers the players to random maps is here, as well as the Interface that selects maps.

-The Bar: This is the largest room in the map, it houses the shops and level up interfaces.

-The Zoo: This is where special items the player find goes. Specifically, npcs and custom props that the players decide they want to keep.

-The Theater: The introductory exposition scene takes place here, and beyond that has no use.

-The Green Room: Jazz Cat events happen here, in an isolated chunk of level not normally accessible. It is a backdrop for conversations and that’s it.

Mechanics Scope

This section is for laying out everything that needs to be made for the game to work as intended, and for any problems that are unsolved.

Orb Mechanics

For the main objective of collecting orbs, we need the following:

        -A system to programatically distribute orb shards are locations that are as “safe” as we can find. By safe, I mean simply that the player can reach it period.

        -Code that can tell where it should spawn the Completed Orb when the players get all 5 shards. Playtesting and design discussion will tell us how and where to try and place the completed orb for collection/

        -A reroll system that costs 1 orb shard, but respawns all the remaining shards + the one the players spent in new locations. Placement rules for new shard locations is left up to the programming team, i've got no idea how to deal with this.

        -This is probably gonna be the hardest part since we have to somehow read the level before we can start making calls about where to slam shit down.

        -Each shard gives 500 jazz dollars to the player that collected it, NOT the entire team. We will need to figure out how to stop people from re-rolling forever and grinding out points personally. Maybe make re-rolling a team wide decision?

        -The Completed Orb gives 1000 jazz dollars to the entire team no matter what upon collection.

More orbs if the map is larger

        Problems:

        -How do we determine the exit bus location upon map completion?

        -How do we stop the game from dropping a shard in the 3d skybox, or some other inaccessible location?

        -Do we let the players ping shard locations on a cooldown so they can tell if a shard DID drop somewhere out of play?

        -How do we determine where shards drop on a reroll?

Exploration Tools:

Exploration tools are the answer to how players deal with level design in a context that it was never created for. Example: the team plays a l4d map and usually there’s a spot where a trolley comes across a chasm slowly while they fight off a horde. For obvious reasons, this will halt progression, so instead we need to enable the mindset of scavenging for resources and some basic tools that will let them circumvent broken design. Here is the preliminary list that is completely up for adjustment and change:

        -A series of basic ramps and boards that maybe can be welded together to make structures or bridges, or at least stacked on top of each other to make towers to climb.

        -Abstract mechanisms that cause a very specific thing to happen, example: a pad that will always just launch whatever props are on it up very fast and very far into the sky. Obvious uses for this are making dangerous elevators, or cannons to launch players a very far distance across the map to ease travel times on larger RP maps.

                

-We’ll need to brainstorm more of these later at a team pow wow.

-Should we let them buy items for spawning that they have sitting in the collection room?

-Vehicles that are very cheap and can be spawned per the amount of them you’ve bought. Example: I buy 4 jalopys, so that means i can spawn 4 of them anywhere for free until i need to restock.

        -We should make some really weird fun vehicles like gravity car and stuff.

        -Do we tie these back into the level up system and let them drive super            airboats?

-Toolguns that allow for movement that manipulating the world with normal hl2 methods doesn’t allow. Example: a line gun that lets you make a zip wire between any two walls as long as they are within a given distance of each other. There’s no other way to do horizontal movement easily in mid air, so this is a use case where it’s a good idea to give them a special gun that allows it.

Absolutely off limits:

Grappling Hooks that just pull you towards where you are looking.

Jetpacks. Might as well give em noclip at this point.

Double jump: this is a cop out and makes it too easy to kz mod your way around the level. (back on the table, actually)

Asset Acquisition Tools:

Asset acquisition tools are how the players heist shit from levels they visit. Anything that is a prop_physics is fair game for theft, and upgrading the way you do it will allow you to steal more things at once, or for a higher return. The initial inspiration is how MGSV lets you kidnap literally anything for profit and gives you a funny visual of a balloon dragging whatever you want infinitely high into the sky. If we can one up this somehow we should, but I think it’s at least a good starting point for visual aesthetics.

        -How do the players tag what they want to take?

        -Is there a limit to how much they can take from one map?

        -Does upgrading this gun make the kidnapping visuals funnier or weirder?

        -What if two players fight over the same asset?

        -Do NPC’s give you special benefits if they are unique characters (eli, alyx, gman)?

Dialogue system:

The Dialogue system exists for the Jazz Cat events, and for the opening and finale. This is basically a bunch of hammer entities that the mapping team can use to hook up cutscenes and relay I/O signals from hammer to the gamemode so they can talk to each other.

        -We need a way to display text in a box, and then press a button to advance it.

        -In hammer I need to be able to fire off inputs when the player advances to the next dialogue box. This will be used for playing animations on a prop_dynamic, timing audio elements to animations, and changing camera angles.

        -Upon scene completion (all dialogue boxes are done and the conversation is over) I need to be able to tell some sort of progression handler that the players Saw An Event, and then tell the game via hammer to advance progression for a specific Jazz Cat.

        -I also need to be able to somehow hide event starts from the player until they are ready to see it. See Sunabouzu’s Shameful Dating Sim Corner for context.

Sunabouzu’s Shameful Dating Sim Corner

I will freely admit to this section being non-gameplay important but I strongly believe this is how we get players to stay here for good until the end and to make it something special. Each jazz cat has 6 unique conversation scenes, and every 2 scenes (every 1 scene?) they unlock a beneficial gameplay modifier.

Part 1: Meeting requirements

Before they can see any scene the player must meet an arbitrary set of requirements per-cat, per-scene. The requirements are always the same, but it’ll be stupid shit like steal 10 Combine or 5 Zombies. This is so we make them have secondary objectives for exploring a map, and incentivize them to actually hunt around the map to go above and beyond for extra content.

        -Do we let the players check what they need to get at any point in time or do we make them talk to the cat first and just remember what they need?

        -What if they never play a map that has something specific they need to advance a jazz cat’s relationship?

        -We should definitely make debug commands to advance progression so we can test this without losing our goddamn brains.

Part 2: Talking to the cat

After they clear the requirements to start a scene, the player must find the Jazz Cat they wanna date in the bar. Upon confirmation that they want to start a scene, the dialogue system takes over and we fire off I/O systems that lock the player into a cutscene mode until the dialogue is done and they’ve received their benefit.

        -How do we stop EVERY player from also watching this? I don’t think the hammer camera entity natively lets me selectively choose who sees it. Maybe we just place the entities down in hammer, name them, and let the code adopt it’s fov/location/rotation to simplify the dialogue code.

        -What happens when someone is in a scene for everybody else? Do they just disappear or stand there and stare off into the middle distance?

Part 3: Benefits

Upon completion of a scene hammer will tell the game to progress the relationship level between that specific player and the specific Jazz Cat they talked to. There is no other way to get these benefits other than talking to cats.

        -The benefits should be specific to the cat. Example: one makes it easier to get money, one unlocks new weirder tools to use, one lets you ping orb shards more frequently, etc etc.

        -The exact benefits are up for debate, but they should be meaningful if not outright fun to acquire.

        -The benefits are per-player, and not team wide(?).

Part 4: The Cast

Bartender Cat: The leader, and always manning the bar. Is the defacto shop keeper, but can be called away to have a chat. Female

Singer Cat: is the vocalist of the jazz band. Female.

Pianist Cat: plays the piano. Male

Chello Cat: plays the cello. Male

Cat personalities are up to whoever writes this, but it should be entertaining in any case. Yes this is incredibly vague, but it’s better to wing it than come up with boring first-thought personalities and locking ourselves into that.
